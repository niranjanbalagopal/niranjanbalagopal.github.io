Perspective

Understanding ecosystems through the lens of disruption and adaptation

I am a wildlife biologist interested in how landscape-scale disruptions alter ecosystem trajectories and animal behavior. My journey in ecology has taken me from the Southern Western Ghats to the high-altitude forests of the Himalayas.

I am currently a PhD candidate at Ashoka University, where I study ecological succession, forest dynamics and habitat suitability in the Himalayan mid hills. My professional background is rooted in understanding climate change, natural evolution of forests and human-animal coexistence. I enjoy using mixed method approaches with a field ecology backbone supplemented with remote sensing and statistical modelling.

Outside of work, I enjoy motorcycling, travelling, weekend binging video games and of course reading.

Professional Fieldwork

Conservation work across India's biodiversity hotspots

Project Officer 2022 — 2023
WWF-India | Southern Western Ghats (Coimbatore & Nilgiris)

Led initiatives for human-elephant conflict mitigation, which involved analyzing conflict drivers and mapping critical elephant use areas. A significant part of my work included the radio-collaring and monitoring of wild elephants using VHF tracking to better understand their movement through human-dominated landscapes.

Junior Field Researcher 2021 — 2022
AERF | Northern Western Ghats (Ratnagiri)

Conducted intensive biodiversity surveys and resource assessments, focusing on habitat health and community-led conservation in the Maharashtra region.

Research Intern 2021
FERAL | Eastern Ghats (Southern Karnataka)

Studied the dynamics of resource sharing among elephants at waterholes, examining how different social groups and individuals utilized limited water sources across numerous landscape sites.
